<p><p>The nanoporous market includes materials like zeolites, clays, silica gel, activated alumina, and others, each with unique properties. Zeolites are crystalline aluminosilicates used in catalysis and gas separation. Clays, natural or modified, are cost-effective adsorbents for purification. Silica gel, a porous form of silicon dioxide, excels in moisture control and chromatography. Activated alumina, derived from aluminum oxide, is ideal for drying and fluoride removal. Other materials include metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) and carbon-based structures, offering advanced applications in energy storage and molecular sieving.</p></p>
